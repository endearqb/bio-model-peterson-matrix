## Overview

This tutorial provides a systematic and in-depth exploration of wastewater biological treatment modeling, with a focus on the **Peterson Rate Matrix** as the unifying mathematical framework. Starting from microbial metabolism fundamentals, it progressively covers the ASM model family (ASM1, ASM2d, ASM3), kinetic theory, COD fractionation, Python-based implementation, and model calibration — offering both theoretical rigor and practical engineering applicability.

### What You Will Learn

- The mathematical structure and physical interpretation of the Peterson Rate Matrix
- How to read, derive, and implement ASM1/ASM2d/ASM3 models from first principles
- COD fractionation methods and their significance in model setup
- Monod kinetics, stoichiometry, and mass balance in biological systems
- Python-based numerical simulation of activated sludge processes
- Practical model calibration and sensitivity analysis workflows
- Current frontiers in wastewater modeling research
